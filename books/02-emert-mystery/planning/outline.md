# Book 2: Cold Impressions - Omaha Case
## High-Level Chapter Outline

### CASE SUMMARY

**Victim**: Michael "Mike" Palmer, 42, real estate agent in Omaha, Nebraska
**Date**: January 9, 2013
**Crime**: Stabbed 19 times in upscale house during property showing, body staged in bathtub with shower running
**Killer**: "Steven" - disguised perpetrator who posed as potential buyer

**THE TRUTH**:
- "Steven" is really Steven Bennett, forensic accountant from Colorado
- Steven was casually dating Laura Hartfield at Creighton University (early 1990s)
- Mike Palmer (casual acquaintance of Steven's) dated Laura briefly, ended the relationship
- Laura was devastated; eventually married Steven but struggled with depression for 20 years
- Steven believed Laura never got over Mike - that Mike "took her twice" (first when they dated, then poisoned the marriage)
- Laura committed suicide November 2012
- Steven blamed Mike for Laura's death and spent 2-3 months planning the perfect murder
- Steven studied other unsolved real estate killings to make it look like a professional hit
- Mike's confusion during murder: didn't recognize disguised Steven until final moments

**INVESTIGATION ARC**:
- Brandon's initial impression: Palmer's confusion leads to "mistaken identity" theory (red herring)
- Chapter 5: Brandon's refined impression reveals Palmer recognized killer in final moments
- Matt's methodical detective work traces Palmer's past to Creighton, then to Laura, then to Steven
- Detective Morrison provides crucial local resources and coordination throughout
- Partnership emphasized: Brandon's ability + Matt's methodology + Morrison's resources = solution

### CHAPTER 12 (Matt POV)
**Tuesday, February 12, 2013**
- Omaha PD interrogation room
- Steven Bennett, handcuffed, calm
- Matt, Brandon, and Morrison present (Morrison leads as primary)
- Steven waives right to attorney: "I want to tell you what happened. I need someone to understand."
- The confession (Morrison recording):
  - **The backstory:** Steven and Laura dating casually at Creighton, Mike Palmer in their social circle
  - "I introduced them. I brought Mike into our friend group. That was my mistake."
  - Laura fell for Mike, it was obvious to everyone
  - Mike and Laura dated briefly (few months), Mike ended it
  - "Laura was devastated. She dropped out. Her whole life trajectory changed because of him."
  - Steven was there to pick up the pieces
  - They married in 1994: "I thought I'd won. I thought she'd chosen me."
  - But 20 years of marriage: "She was never fully there. I could see it. In quiet moments, in photos, in the way she talked about college. Mike Palmer was the ghost in our marriage."
  - Laura's depression: "I tried to help. Therapy, medication, everything. But nothing worked because the problem wasn't clinical. The problem was Mike Palmer."
  - Laura's suicide, November 2012: "I found her in the bathtub. Just like I found Mike."
  - "And I knew. I knew it was Mike's fault. He took her from me twice."
- **The planning:**
  - Steven researched for weeks: "I needed to do it right. No mistakes. Nothing that would lead back to Laura."
  - Studied the other real estate killings: "I wanted it to look professional. Random. A serial killer who targets agents."
  - Practiced the disguise, the accent, the limp
  - Scouted Omaha in December: "I learned Mike's routine. Found the perfect property. Planned every detail."
  - January 9, 2013: "I met Mike at the mall. He didn't recognize me. Why would he? I was just some guy from college 25 years ago. Barely friends."
- **The murder:**
  - "I talked to him about tile choices. About fixtures. Normal real estate conversation."
  - "Then I killed him. Quick, efficient. Like I'd planned."
  - "But at the end, right before he died, he looked at me and he knew. 'Steven?' he said. Just my name, like a question."
  - "And I said, 'For Laura.' That's all. 'For Laura.'"
- **The aftermath:**
  - Steven drove Palmer's car to the shopping center, switched to his rental
  - Disposed of phone and wallet in separate locations
  - Returned to Colorado, went through motions for a few weeks
  - "But it didn't change anything. Laura was still dead. Mike was dead. And I felt... empty."
- Morrison asks: "Do you think Laura really carried a torch for Mike Palmer all those years?"
- Steven, honest: "I don't know. Maybe. Maybe not. Maybe I convinced myself she did because I needed someone to blame. Maybe the depression was just depression. I'll never know."
- Matt: "Mike Palmer didn't deserve to die for a college relationship."
- Steven: "I know. But I needed him to pay. Someone had to pay."
- Confession concludes, Steven formally charged
- Case closed

**Epilogue reflection (Matt's internal thoughts):**
- This case required all three of them: Brandon's impression, Matt's methodology, Morrison's resources
- Neither federal agent could have solved it alone
- Partnership is what makes them effective
- Steven Bennett will be convicted - justice served, but hollow
- Two people dead, one life destroyed, all because of a brief college romance 25 years ago

- Methodical, systematic approach

- Equal partner, not subordinate
- Provides crucial local resources and coordination

**Pacing:**

## WRITING NOTES

**Dialogue Style:**
- Spare, pulp detective novel feel
- Characters say what they mean, minimal exposition
- Professional shorthand between Matt and Brandon
- Morrison's dialogue shows competence without explaining it

**Scene Structure:**
- Keep scenes focused and purposeful
- Each scene should advance investigation or develop character
- Avoid filler or unnecessary description
- Trust reader to follow without over-explaining

**POV Guidelines:**
- Brandon chapters: more internal, psychological observations
- Matt chapters: more external, analytical observations
- Both remain professional, focused on the work
- Personal moments rare but meaningful

**Morrison Characterization:**
- Competent, professional, not defensive
- Knows when to ask for help
- Values solving the case over ego
- Detective's instincts and experience evident in actions
- No romantic subplots or unnecessary personal details
- Respected colleague, not subordinate

**Brandon's Ability:**
- Keep it ambiguous: psychic or subconscious profiling?
- Physical/emotional toll evident but not dramatized
- Provides insights, not answers
- Can be wrong or misleading (mistaken identity theory)
- Requires refinement (second visit to crime scene)
- Matt treats it seriously but pragmatically

**Geography/Setting:**
- Omaha, Nebraska: Midwest professionalism, no frills
- Winter setting (January/February): cold, gray, matches tone
- Hampton Inn, police stations, suburbs: functional spaces
- Colorado trip: different setting but same procedural focus

**Procedural Accuracy:**
- Warrants required for financial records, therapy notes
- Miranda rights at arrest
- Jurisdiction matters (Morrison leads arrest in her city)
- FBI assists but doesn't take over
- Database searches, records requests take time
- Professional courtesy between agencies
