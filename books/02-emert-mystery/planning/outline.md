## Physical Identifier

Needs to be:
- Memorable enough that witnesses recall it
- Distinctive enough that Matt/Brandon recognize him on sight
- But plausible as disguise for a professional

**Options:**
1. **Severe facial scar** (knife or burn) - hard to hide, memorable
2. **Milky/damaged eye** - distinctive, suggests past violence
3. **Missing fingers on one hand** - visible when shaking hands/signing papers
4. **Pronounced Eastern European accent** (voice not appearance)
5. **Unusually tall** (6'5"+) - can't disguise height

**My recommendation:** Facial scar + accent combo. Gives multiple witness touchpoints.

### **PROLOGUE (Non-POV, Present Tense)**

**Setting:** January 2013, Omaha, Nebraska, around 1:00 PM

**Structure:**
1. Homeowner returns to check on property (new construction showing)
2. Notices real estate agent's Lexus still in driveway (strange - showing should be over)
3. Front door slightly ajar
4. Enters cautiously, calls out
5. Finds blood trail leading upstairs
6. Bathroom: victim in tub, shower running, multiple stab wounds
7. Victim's expensive watch and wedding ring missing from body
8. 911 call

**Tone:** Clinical, procedural, building dread. Focus on details that will matter later.

**End:** First responders arrive, scene is chaos, but it's clear this wasn't random.

**Scene 2: Matt's Methodology**

### **CHAPTER 3 (Brandon POV)**

**Setting:** Omaha Police Department, meeting Detective Morrison

**Opening:**
- Modern police headquarters, much bigger than Parke County
- Detective Claire Morrison waiting in conference room
- Late 30s, sharp suit, murder book already organized on table
- Brandon notices immediately: she's prepared, professional, no-nonsense

**Scene 1: Initial Meeting**
- Morrison stands, firm handshake: "Agents. Claire Morrison, Homicide."
- Matt: "Matt Ellis. Brandon Gray. Thanks for coordinating with us."
- Morrison: "Let's be clear up front - I requested FBI assistance. This isn't about jurisdiction or ego. This case needs fresh eyes and federal resources."
- Brandon notes: Direct. Confident. Knows what she needs.
- Morrison walks them through her investigation - it's thorough, methodical
- Wife and business partner vetted extensively, cleared
- Financial deep-dive: nothing suspicious
- Background checks: no enemies, no threats, no debts
- Victim was well-regarded, successful, stable marriage, no vices
- Mall security footage reviewed: "Steven" kept face away from cameras (professional)
- Morrison: "Whoever did this knew what they were doing. And I've hit every wall there is."

**Scene 2: Brandon Studies Crime Scene Photos**
- Morrison spreads photos across table
- Crime scene documentation is meticulous
- Brandon studying victim's position, blood patterns, staging
- Morrison watches him work, doesn't interrupt
- Matt asks questions, Morrison answers with precise details
- Brandon getting impressions from photos but incomplete
- Victim was confused, not just afraid
- Like he didn't understand why this was happening

**Scene 3: First Theory**
- Brandon: "Detective, did you consider the possibility this was the wrong victim?"
- Morrison pauses, considers: "Mistaken identity? We looked at similar names, same development. Nothing jumped out. Why?"
- Brandon: "Something about the victim's... state. Like this didn't make sense to him."
- Morrison: "That's interesting. Most victims are confused when they're being murdered."
- Brandon: "Not confused about dying. Confused about *why*."
- Matt: "Worth exploring. Could explain the lack of obvious motive."
- Morrison nods: "All right. I'll pull similar cases, comparable victims. We can review tomorrow."
- Then: "But first - you want to see the actual scene, don't you Agent Gray?"
- Brandon looks at her: She's sharp. Picked up on it.
- Brandon: "Yes."
- Morrison: "I'll get us access. Tomorrow morning, 9 AM. Property's been released but owner's cooperative."

**End:** They check into hotel. Tomorrow they visit the scene. Brandon's already feeling the weight of what they'll find there.
